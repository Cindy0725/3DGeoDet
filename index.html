Abstract

This paper proposes ImGeoDet, a novel geometry-aware 3D object detection approach from single-view or multi-view posed RGB images of indoor scenes. The key challenge for image-based 3D object detection tasks is the lack of 3D geometry cues, which leads to ambiguity in establishing correspondences between images and 3D representations. To tackle this problem, ImGeoDet utilizes depth information predicted by a light-weight depth estimation network to generate efficient 3D geometric representation to guide the network. Specifically, We utilize the predicted depth information to learn voxel occupancy and optimize the voxelized 3D feature volume accordingly. To further enhance the 3D awareness of the feature volume, we integrate it with an implicit 3D representation, truncated signed distance function. While improving the comprehension of 3D geometry by utilizing 3D representation, our approach retains the ability to achieve end-to-end training. Our approach surpasses the performance of state-of-the-art image-based methods by achieving a 9.0 mAP@0.5 improvement on the SUN RGB-D benchmark and a 3.3 mAP@0.5 improvement on the ScanNetV2 dataset.

Results on SUN RGB-D Benchmark

ImGeoDet outperforms ImvoxelNet by 25.4% and 73.5% in mAP@0.25 and mAP0.5. Moreover, it significantly narrows down the performance gap between the monocular detection methods and point cloud-based detection methods (VoteNet).
